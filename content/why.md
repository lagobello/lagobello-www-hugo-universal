# Why Lago Bello

Lago Bello is a gated residential subdivision in Brownsville, TX. Fifty-two lots
arranged around a private 16-acre
freshwater lake, behind a single staffed gate, with the kind of
infrastructure most subdivisions in the Rio Grande Valley don't have.

Here's the short version of who it's for and what makes it different.

## A Lake You Actually Own

The 16-acre freshwater lake at the center of the community is the only
one of its kind in Brownsville. It's privately owned by the lot owners
through the HOA — not a city pond, not a leased reservoir. Lakefront
homeowners have the right to build a private dock at no extra charge.
Combustion-free watercraft only, so it stays quiet.

[See waterfront lots →](/waterfront/)

## Walk-to-School Convenience

Lago Bello is the closest residential subdivision to South Texas
Independent School District's Medical Professions campus. For families
attending that school, the morning carpool line is a sidewalk.

[Read more about the schools →](/school/)

## Infrastructure That Saves Money Later

Every lot is delivered with:

- **Buried electrical** (Brownsville PUB) — no overhead lines, fewer
  storm outages.
- **Omni Fiber** pre-installed to the lot — gigabit-class internet from
  day one.
- **Municipal water and wastewater**.
- **28-foot paved streets** with ADA sidewalks.
- **Storm drainage** and a retention wall along the lake boundary.

A lot that's already serviced this way avoids one of the larger hidden
costs of new construction.

[Builder specs →](/builders/)

## Located in a Flood-Zone-X Pocket

Lago Bello sits in FEMA Zone X and X-shaded — a meaningfully better
classification than most of the surrounding Brownsville flood plain.
That means no federal mandate for flood insurance, which can save
thousands a year over a typical 30-year mortgage.

(Maps are subject to change; the [interactive map](/interactive-map/)
shows the current flood-hazard layer.)
